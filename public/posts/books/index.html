Books

January 12, 2023 2-minute read

Here are some of my favorite books I have read (mostly listend to): Link to heading

1. Outliers by Malcolm Gladwell Link to heading

Outliers by Malcolm Gladwell is a really interesting book that shows how success isn’t just about being really talented or working really hard - it’s also about having the right opportunities and circumstances.

2. The Creative Act: A Way of Being by Rick Rubin Link to heading

The Creative Act by Rick Rubin is an inspiring essay that offers valuable insights into the creative process, emphasizing the importance of authenticity, collaboration, and experimentation in unlocking one’s creative potential.

3. Blink: The Power of Thinking Without Thinking by Malcolm Gladwell Link to heading

Blink by Malcolm Gladwell is a thought-provoking book that explores how our brains make split-second decisions and judgments, drawing on examples from psychology, neuroscience, and real-world situations to argue that sometimes our unconscious mind can be more effective than deliberate thinking.

4. Naked Statstics by Charles Wheelan Link to heading

Naked Statistics by Charles Wheelan is a book that explains how to understand and use statistics in real life. It shows you how statistics can be used to make better decisions, and teaches you how to identify common mistakes people make when interpreting data.

5. The Tipping Point by Malcolm Gladwell Link to heading

The Tipping Point by Malcolm Gladwel (yes another Gladwell book) is a fascinating book that explores how ideas and products can “catch on” and spread like viruses, drawing on examples from a wide range of fields to explain how the right conditions can make a big difference.
